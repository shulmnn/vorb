# Speech-to-text product references

## Category patterns

The strongest speech-to-text product pages explain the writing outcome before the recording mechanism:

- Show familiar destinations—email, notes, messages, documents, and prompts—so people immediately understand where the transcript goes.
- Make the transformation explicit with speech, a waveform, and visible text rather than relying on a microphone or abstract orb alone.
- Lead with a short benefit such as faster writing, capturing thoughts, privacy, or working in every app.
- Use product UI later to prove configuration, privacy, model choice, and workflow control.

## Products reviewed

- [Wispr Flow](https://wisprflow.ai/) frames dictation as turning thoughts into writing in any app and demonstrates familiar work contexts.
- [Aqua Voice](https://aquavoice.com/windows) leads with voice typing in everyday apps and makes the written result central to the story.
- [Willow](https://willowvoice.com/) connects natural speech to finished writing and organizes examples around concrete use cases.
- [MacWhisper](https://www.macwhisper.net/) makes the file-to-transcript outcome explicit and foregrounds local privacy.
- [VoiceInk](https://www.voice-ink.com/) pairs system-wide dictation with clear local/cloud model choices and privacy messaging.
- [TalkTastic](https://talktastic.com/) shows tap-to-talk behavior inside recognizable writing workflows.
- [Superwhisper](https://superwhisper.com/) leads with a concrete shortcut, a voice-to-text outcome, and familiar destinations such as Slack, Gmail, and Notes.

## Decisions for Vorb

1. Treat the orb as a compact recording-state indicator, not the main explanation of the product.
2. Use the first screenshot to show a human thought becoming typed text.
3. Use the next two screenshots to show the transformation and its destinations explicitly.
4. Follow with native screens that prove local Whisper, bring-your-own-provider support, model control, history, and shortcut behavior.
5. Avoid claims about rewriting, polishing, or filler removal; Vorb currently transcribes and delivers text rather than editing it.
